About Us

Amogh is the first series of Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs) being developed by the students of IIT Madras, in association with Centre for Innovation. A multi-disciplinary team comprising of undergraduate and postgraduate students, has been working towards development of an underwater vehicle with maneuverability and control capability. Starting in July 2012 after successfully testing a ROV, the vehicle design has been iterated envisioning a full fledged AUV. The team actively participates in various national and international competitions, conferences and exhibitions and has gained tremendous support from institute professors, scientists and industrialists of reputed organizations.


  • IIT Madras

    Indian Institute of Technology Madras is one among the foremost institutes of national importance in higher technological education, basic and applied research.

  • Centre for Innovation

    Centre for Innovation (CFI) is a "Student Lab" at IIT Madras. It provides students the necessary platform for realizing their ideas. A great workspace, comprehensive inventory, ample guidance from faculty & senior-students, the zeal and passion of the CFI team make CFI a gifted asset for the institute students.

  • AUVSI Robosub

    Robosub competition is organised annually by AUVSI at SSC Pacific Transdec, San Diego, California, USA. The goals of the competition is to provide opportunities for students to experience the challenges of system engineering, to develop skill in accomplishing realistic missions with autonomous vehicles and to foster relationships between young engineers and the organizations developing and producing autonomous vehicle technologies.

  • NIOT SAVe

    National Institute of Ocean Technology (NIOT) , under the Ministry of Earth Sciences, organizes a competition for students pursuing engineering degree to visualize and design an autonomous underwater vehicle. The conceptual basis for Student Autonomous underwater Vehicle (SAVe) is a highly mobile AUV, to be built based on engineering principles.
